Description

“Applied Research” is an intensive 5-day course that provides graduate students with a comprehensive perspective on the practice of scientific research in both academic and industrial contexts. Through interactive sessions combining theoretical foundations, computational tools, experimental equipment design, and real-world case studies, students will develop practical skills to execute research projects efficiently and creatively.

The course features presentations from academic researchers and industry professionals who will share their career trajectories and perspectives, offering students direct insight into different professional paths in applied science. With emphasis on hands-on methodologies, computational data analysis, and collaborative problem-solving, this course prepares students to face the real challenges of modern research.

This course is highly participatory. Students are expected to come with an open mind, actively participate in discussions, ask questions, and engage with both the instructor and guest presenters.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Design and plan low-budget laboratory equipment applying fundamental scientific principles and accessible fabrication tools
  2. Process and analyze experimental data using computational tools (Python, IgorPro) to extract quantitative conclusions and visualize results
  3. Develop complete research workflows integrating experimental design, data acquisition, computational analysis, and communication of results
  4. Evaluate the applicability of computational simulation techniques and data acquisition systems (Arduino, LabVIEW) to specific research problems
  5. Structure scientific documents using appropriate tools (LaTeX) and effectively communicate technical ideas in academic and industrial contexts
  6. Contrast methodological approaches between academic and industrial research to propose creative solutions to complex scientific problems

Evaluation

Grading Components

Component Weight Description
Final Evaluation 50% Individual test with specific questions on technical content and open questions where students propose complete workflows for research problems
Active Participation 20% Continuous evaluation of participation in discussions, practical sessions, and interactive activities
Group Work 30% Collaborative project developing a proposal to solve an applied research problem, including experimental design and data analysis strategy
